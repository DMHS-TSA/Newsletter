October Newsletter

November 4, 2025

This Month in Tech

October delivered a shockwave of progress across the technology landscape, defined by strategic spending on computational power, genuine breakthroughs in materials science, and a critical shift in workforce composition.

The Compute Core: AI Hardware Gets Radical

Apple Just Put an AI Accelerator in Every GPU Core

Apple's launch of the M5 chip on October 15, 2025, confirmed that local, on-device AI processing is now a mandatory feature for premium hardware.

  • Key Shift: The new third-generation 3nm M5 chip features a Neural Accelerator in every core of its 10-core GPU. This moves AI acceleration away from the dedicated Neural Engine and embeds high-speed generative AI capabilities across the entire graphics subsystem.
  • Performance: The M5 delivers up to 3.5x faster AI performance than the M4 chip and increases unified memory bandwidth by nearly 30% to 153GB/s —essential for running powerful Large Language Models (LLMs) locally with negligible latency.

The $100 Billion AI Compute Land Grab

The month kicked off with an unprecedented surge in capital commitment, pushing total infrastructure spending on AI compute past $100 billion in a matter of days.

  • The Stakes: Hyperscale cloud providers, including Microsoft, characterized the race for securing specialized GPUs and data centers as a "land grab". This extreme demand confirms that hardware scarcity and energy demands will keep AI compute costs high for years.
  • Vertical Integration: On October 31, NVIDIA announced a partnership with Samsung Electronics to construct a new AI factory powered by over 50,000 NVIDIA GPUs. This collaboration will use advanced AI tools to accelerate and optimize the very production of future semiconductor chips.

Quantum & Materials: Beyond Classical Limits

Google Claims ‘Verifiable Quantum Advantage’

On October 22, Google achieved a major milestone in quantum computing, successfully demonstrating "Verifiable Quantum Advantage".

  • The Breakthrough: Using a novel algorithm dubbed "Quantum Echoes," the quantum computer successfully computed the structure of a molecule.
  • The Metric: It ran the calculation an extraordinary 13,000 times faster than the best available classical algorithm running on a world-class supercomputer. This achievement validates a path toward scalable quantum verification and useful scientific simulation.

Superconducting Germanium Revolutionizes Quantum Manufacturing

In materials science, researchers reported the successful transformation of Germanium, a common semiconductor, into a superconductor.

  • This achievement, which had eluded scientists for over six decades, is profound because it unifies the foundational building blocks of classical electronics (semiconductors) and quantum systems (superconductors).

  • The discovery creates a straightforward pathway for scalable, "foundry-ready" quantum devices, accelerating the manufacturing timeline for hybrid quantum circuits and low-power cryogenic electronics.

Space & Infrastructure: LEO Becomes a Utility

SpaceX Hits Triple Digits: 100th Starlink Launch of the Year

On October 31, SpaceX achieved a substantial operational milestone by successfully completing its 100th Starlink mission of 2025.

  • Industrial Scale: The mission, which deployed 28 Starlink V2 Mini satellites, was the company’s 15th orbital launch during October alone.

  • Reuse Record: The Falcon 9 booster used for the mission, B1063, completed its 29th flight. This relentless, high-cadence reuse confirms that access to Low Earth Orbit (LEO) is fully industrialized and cost-optimized.

Workforce & Risk: The Automation Shockwave

AI-Driven Layoffs Accelerate Corporate Restructuring

The technology sector is in the midst of a structural realignment driven by AI productivity gains.

  • The Stats: Over the course of 2025, 112,732 tech employees lost their jobs across 218 companies, largely attributed by industry leaders to the rapid adoption of artificial intelligence and automation.
  • Corporate Impact: This was epitomized in late October when Amazon announced cuts of 14,000 corporate positions—nearly 4% of its entire corporate base—across AWS, operations, and human resources. The rise of specialized AI, such as Google’s Gemini model achieving State-of-the-Art performance in Graphical User Interface (GUI) automation, validates the rapid automability of complex white-collar tasks.

Supply Chain Cyber Attacks Hit Record High

October was marked by an alarming escalation in systemic cyber risk. The month recorded a record surge in software supply chain attacks, peaking at 41 claimed incidents—over 30% higher than the previous record.

  • Target Profile: IT and IT services companies remain the most targeted sector (107 attacks year-to-date) because compromising one grants high-leverage access to hundreds of downstream targets.

  • Compounding Risk: This threat is compounded by the fact that many mission-critical industrial control systems still operate on dangerously outdated platforms, such as Windows XP.

Foundry Roadmap: TSMC Pushes 1.4nm

Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co. (TSMC) secured crucial construction permits in late October for its state-of-the-art A14 wafer fab in Taichung, Taiwan.

  • The A14 node, representing the 1.4-nanometer process, is targeted for mass production in the second half of 2028.
  • The new node is expected to deliver 15% higher performance or consume 30% less power compared to the impending 2nm (N2) node, which is scheduled to begin volume production before the end of 2025.
